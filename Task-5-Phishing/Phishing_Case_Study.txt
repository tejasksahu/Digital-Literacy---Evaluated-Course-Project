Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing
________________________________________
What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which an attacker impersonates a legitimate, trusted organisation — such as a bank, university, government body, or popular app — to trick a victim into voluntarily revealing sensitive information such as passwords, one-time passwords (OTPs), bank account numbers, or credit card details.
________________________________________
Case Study: The Fake Bank Alert
Scenario (Realistic Fictional Incident)
Rahul, a 20-year-old engineering student in Bhopal, received an SMS one morning that appeared to be from his bank. The message read:
“ALERT: Suspicious activity detected on your SBI account. Your account will be blocked in 24 hours. Verify now: http://sbi-secure-verify.in/login”
The message created a sense of urgency and fear. Rahul clicked the link, which opened a webpage that looked identical to the official SBI NetBanking login page — same logo, same layout, same colour scheme. He entered his username, password, and then his OTP when prompted.
Within minutes, Rs. 18,500 was transferred out of his account. The website was a fake — a clone created by attackers hosted on a domain designed to look legitimate at first glance.
________________________________________
How It Happened – Step by Step
1.	Attacker creates a fake domain that closely resembles a trusted bank’s website.
2.	Bulk SMS is sent to thousands of numbers with a fear-inducing message and a link to the fake site.
3.	Victim clicks the link due to urgency and lands on the cloned page.
4.	Victim enters credentials — username, password, and OTP.
5.	Attacker captures the credentials in real time and immediately logs into the real banking portal.
6.	Funds are transferred before the victim realises what happened.
________________________________________
Who is Typically Targeted?
•	College students and young adults who bank online frequently
•	Elderly users unfamiliar with phishing tactics
•	People in a hurry or stressed when they receive the message
•	Anyone who does not verify the URL before entering credentials
________________________________________
Consequences
•	Immediate financial loss (often non-recoverable if not reported within 72 hours)
•	Compromise of online accounts if the same password is reused elsewhere
•	Identity theft if personal documents were shared on the fake site
•	Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital financial systems
